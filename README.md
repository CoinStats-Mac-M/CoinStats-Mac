## 📄 Overview

<a href="#coinStats">CoinStats</a> serves cryptocurrency holders who have assets across multiple exchanges and wallets and want a single view of their complete portfolio, active traders who need price alerts and market monitoring, and DeFi participants who want their decentralized positions integrated alongside centralized holdings.

The <a href="#coinStats">multi-platform portfolio consolidation use case</a> is CoinStats' primary value — the cryptocurrency holder with accounts on three exchanges and two hardware wallets who wants to see total portfolio value, asset allocation, and P&L without logging into each platform separately. CoinStats aggregates the complete picture in one dashboard. The <a href="#coinStats">price alert monitoring use case</a> sets specific price targets for potential entry or exit points — getting notified when a target price is reached rather than manually checking prices throughout the day.

The <a href="#coinStats">DeFi position tracking use case</a> includes decentralized finance positions — staking rewards, liquidity pool values, lending positions — in the portfolio view alongside exchange balances, providing a complete picture of all cryptocurrency holdings including assets held in on-chain protocols. <a href="#coinStats">Market awareness use case</a> uses CoinStats' price feed, news aggregation, and market overview for staying informed about the cryptocurrency market without visiting multiple information sources.</p>

<a href="#coinStats">Portfolio benchmarking</a> compares portfolio performance against Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other benchmarks — understanding whether the portfolio has outperformed or underperformed the broader market over selected time periods. <a href="#coinStats">Multiple portfolio support</a> creates separate portfolio views for different purposes — a long-term holding portfolio separate from an active trading portfolio or a DeFi portfolio.

<a href="#coinStats">Free and premium tiers</a> provide basic portfolio tracking at no cost with advanced features including unlimited exchange connections, DeFi tracking, and alerts in premium plans. <a href="#coinStats">CoinStats app availability</a> on Mac alongside iPhone, iPad, and other platforms keeps the portfolio view consistent across all devices for cryptocurrency monitoring wherever the user is.</p>

## 💬 FAQ

<details>
<summary>Can I create multiple portfolios in CoinStats?</summary>

Yes. Multiple portfolio views create separate tracking for different investment strategies or purposes.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does CoinStats aggregate news?</summary>

Yes. A news feed pulls from major cryptocurrency sources with portfolio-relevant filtering that surfaces news about held coins.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I compare my portfolio performance against Bitcoin?</summary>

Yes. Portfolio benchmarking compares performance against Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other benchmarks over selected time periods.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does CoinStats support hardware wallets?</summary>

Hardware wallet balances track by entering the public wallet address — no private key or hardware wallet connection required.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is CoinStats suitable for tax reporting?</summary>

CoinStats is a portfolio tracker and market monitoring tool. For tax calculation and reporting, a dedicated tool like CoinLedger is more appropriate.

</details>
